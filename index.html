Advantages of Power BI

Supports Many Data Sources

Power BI flexibly connects to diverse data sources, including cloud services, files, databases, and online platforms, importing large datasets regardless of structure.

Similarity with Excel

Power BI shares features with Excel, easing the transition for users. It includes Power Query for data connection, reshaping, and publishing via M Language, and Power Pivot with DAX for building models, deriving columns, and calculating KPIs.

Rich Visualizations

Power BI offers immersive, interactive visuals with filtering and drill-down capabilities. Reports consist of individual charts, dashboards integrate multiple reports, and apps combine both reports and dashboards.

Large Data Handling Capabilities

Power BI is known to have hundreds of methods of transforming the data. It can perform aggregations, slicing-dicing and also custom transformations to clean and shape messy data.

Deployment Options

Power BI dashboards and apps are accessible on the web and mobile devices with varying access levels, allowing organizations to control content consumption based on policies and user privileges.

Flexible Collaboration

Power BI Subscriptions enable secure collaboration on dashboards, allowing resource sharing across Workspaces and Capacities. Each user has a personal Workspace for creating and sharing reports.

Shared Datasets

Power BI partners and trusted organizations can publish verified datasets, providing a reliable data source for designers. Updates to source data automatically refresh connected dashboards and apps.
